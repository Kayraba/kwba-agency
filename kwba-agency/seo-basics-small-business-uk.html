5 SEO basics every UK small business should sort out first

By KWBA Agency March 2025 8 min read

Most SEO advice on the internet is written for marketing managers at SaaS companies with five-figure monthly budgets. If you run a plumbing business in Milton Keynes, a salon in Northampton, or an MOT garage in Luton, almost none of it applies to you.

Here's the truth: 90% of the SEO results a UK small business will ever see come from getting five basic things right. If you haven't done these, no amount of link-building or content marketing will save you. If you have, you're already ahead of most of your competitors.

Why this matters: Local search ("plumber near me", "best café Milton Keynes") drives 46% of all Google searches. If you're not appearing in those results, you're invisible to nearly half your potential customers.

01 Google Business Profile

This is the single highest-leverage thing a UK small business can do for search visibility. Google Business Profile (the listing that shows up in the map results) is free, takes 20 minutes to set up, and most of your competitors haven't bothered to optimise it properly.

The minimum standard:

  • Verified business with a real address and phone number
  • Primary category set correctly (not just "service" — be specific)
  • Business hours updated, including bank holidays
  • At least 8–10 photos of your work, premises, or team
  • Reviews. Aim for at least 15 with an average above 4.5
  • Regular Google Posts (treat them like 1-paragraph blog updates)

If you do nothing else from this list, do this. A properly-maintained Google Business Profile alone will outperform a £2,000/month SEO retainer for most local businesses.

02 Schema markup

Schema markup is invisible structured data on your website that tells Google exactly what type of business you are, where you're located, and what you sell. Without it, Google is guessing. With it, Google knows.

For a UK small business website, the schema types that matter most are:

  • LocalBusiness — your name, address, phone, opening hours
  • Organization — your brand identity, logo, social profiles
  • Service — each service you offer, with a clear description
  • Review / AggregateRating — your average rating, displayed in search results as stars
  • FAQPage — if your page has a FAQ section, mark it up

A good web developer will include all of this as standard. If yours didn't, ask why. (Hint: at KWBA every site we build ships with this schema baked in from day one.)

03 Page speed

Google explicitly uses page speed as a ranking factor, especially on mobile. More importantly, your visitors will leave if your site is slow. 40% of visitors abandon a page that takes more than 3 seconds to load.

To check yours, go to pagespeed.web.dev and enter your URL. Look at the mobile score. Aim for 90+. If you're below 70, you have a problem.

The most common page-speed killers on UK small business sites:

  • Massive uncompressed image files (a 4MB hero image will tank your score)
  • Too many WordPress plugins — each one adds loading weight
  • Embedded YouTube videos that auto-load
  • Multiple Google Fonts loaded but never used
  • No image lazy-loading

04 Mobile-first design

Since 2019, Google has used the mobile version of your site as the primary one for indexing. If your site doesn't work properly on a phone, you don't really have a website as far as Google is concerned.

"Mobile-friendly" doesn't mean "the desktop version shrunk down". It means the site was designed for thumbs first: large tap targets, no horizontal scrolling, no popups that cover the screen, no text smaller than 16px, no buttons buried in nested menus.

Test yours on a real phone — not Chrome's mobile-view emulator. Try to book an appointment, find your phone number, or contact you. If it takes more than 3 taps, redesign it.

05 Local keywords on the page

Google can only rank you for a search if your site clearly says you do that thing in that place. The biggest mistake small business sites make is using generic copy that could be on any business's website anywhere in the country.

Fix it by making sure your homepage and service pages explicitly say:

  • What you do (the service)
  • Where you do it (your city, towns served, postcodes covered)
  • Who you do it for (residential, commercial, emergency, scheduled)

Example: "We're an emergency plumber covering Milton Keynes, Bedford, and Northampton — 24/7 callout for residential and commercial properties." That single sentence tells Google more than three pages of generic "professional plumbing services" copy.

One important note: don't keyword-stuff. Don't write "Milton Keynes plumber Milton Keynes plumbing services Milton Keynes emergency Milton Keynes" — Google will penalise that. Write naturally; just make sure the relevant terms appear genuinely in the context they belong in.

What about everything else?

Backlinks. Guest posts. Long-tail keyword research. Topical clusters. Content silos. All of it is real and all of it matters — but only after you've done the five basics above. Spending money on link-building when your Google Business Profile is half-finished and your page speed is 38 is like buying a turbocharger for a car with a flat tyre.

Get the five fundamentals right. Maintain them. Then — and only then — look at the more advanced stuff.
