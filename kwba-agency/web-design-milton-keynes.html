The three mistakes Milton Keynes businesses make

Mistake 1: Using a website builder and calling it done. Wix, Squarespace, and similar platforms are fine for getting something online quickly. But they carry a ceiling. Template-based sites look like template-based sites. They load slowly. They rank poorly in local search. And they don't build trust the way a custom-built site does. If you're serious about your business, your website should reflect that.

Mistake 2: Treating the website as a brochure, not a sales tool. Most business websites are passive — they exist to answer the question "does this company exist?" A good website should answer "why should I choose this company?" and then make it effortless to take the next step. Clear pricing, social proof above the fold, a frictionless contact or booking flow. These aren't nice-to-haves; they're the difference between a visitor and a customer.

Mistake 3: Ignoring local SEO entirely. When someone in Milton Keynes Googles "plumber near me" or "accountant Milton Keynes", the businesses that appear are the ones that have done the basics: Google Business Profile, local keywords on the page, name/address/phone (NAP) consistency, and a few local citations. None of this is technical wizardry — it's maintenance work that most businesses simply haven't done.

The uncomfortable truth: If your website looks worse than your competitor's, you will lose customers to them — even if you're the better business. Perception drives decisions before anyone ever calls you.

What a proper Milton Keynes website should do

A well-built business website in 2025 should:

  • Load in under 2 seconds on mobile — where more than half your visitors will be
  • Appear in Google when local customers search for your service + location
  • Show social proof (testimonials, reviews, project photos) within the first scroll
  • Make it dead easy to contact you, book a call, or get a quote
  • Look professional enough that visitors don't immediately question your credibility
  • Convert — meaning a meaningful percentage of visitors should take an action

None of these are ambitious goals. They're a baseline. And yet the majority of small business websites in Milton Keynes and across the UK fall short on at least three of them.
