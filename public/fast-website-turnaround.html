How we build and launch a professional website in 48 hours

By KWBA Agency March 2025 6 min read

Most agencies take 6–12 weeks to build a small business website. We do it in 48–72 hours. This raises the obvious question: are we cutting corners, or is the rest of the industry padding their timelines?

Honestly — a bit of both. Some agencies genuinely have legitimate reasons for long timelines (multi-stakeholder approval cycles, e-commerce builds with thousands of products, complex integrations). But for a typical small business brochure site, 6 weeks is mostly project-management overhead, sequential workflow, and waiting on clients to send things. None of which is required if you redesign the process.

Here's exactly how a KWBA build runs.

Why most agencies are slow

The standard agency timeline looks like this:

  • Week 1–2: Discovery calls, briefs, stakeholder workshops
  • Week 3–4: Wireframes, design mockups, revisions
  • Week 5–6: More revisions, more meetings
  • Week 7–8: Development begins
  • Week 9–10: Content reviews, copy edits, more revisions
  • Week 11–12: Testing, launch, post-launch fixes

If you removed the waiting time, the actual hands-on-keyboard work is maybe 5–8 days. Everything else is project management, asynchronous email loops, and stakeholder politics. Our process is built to eliminate that overhead.

The KWBA 48-hour timeline

Hour 0 Brief submitted

You fill in a structured brief

Not a 90-minute discovery call. A focused 15-minute form: what your business does, who your customers are, three competitors you like, your existing brand colours (if any), the 5 most important pages, and any text or photos you already have. Submitted online.

Hour 0 — 4 Strategy & structure

We map the architecture & copy direction

From your brief, we plan the site structure (which pages, in what order), the conversion path (how a visitor becomes an enquiry), and the brand direction. No mockups yet — we go straight to a working prototype because it's 10× faster.

Hour 4 — 12 Build

The site is built directly in code

We work from a library of bespoke components we've developed over hundreds of builds — buttons, cards, hero sections, pricing tables, testimonial blocks. Each one is hand-coded but designed to be assembled quickly. The output is a custom site, not a template.

Hour 12 — 18 Copy & content

We write the copy

Most agencies make you write your own copy and wait weeks for you to send it. We write the entire first draft for you, using your brief and a structured copywriting framework. You review and tweak — usually it needs minor changes, not a full rewrite.

Hour 18 — 24 Polish

SEO, schema, page speed, mobile

This is the work that gets skipped on most freelancer builds. We add schema markup, optimise images, run Lighthouse audits, test on three real devices, fix anything that scores below 90 on mobile. This is what makes a site rank and convert, not just exist.

Hour 24 — 36 Your review

You review a live staging URL

You see the actual site, not a mockup. You can click through it, read the copy, test on your phone. Send us a list of changes — usually small things like phone number, photo swaps, copy tweaks.

Hour 36 — 48 Launch

Revisions and go-live

We apply your changes, do a final round of QA, point your domain at the server, configure SSL, set up Google Search Console, submit the sitemap, and the site is live. You get the keys to everything.

What we don't compromise on

Fast doesn't mean rough. Every site we ship includes:

  • Custom design. Not a Wix template with your logo dropped in
  • Mobile-first responsive layout. Tested on real iOS and Android devices
  • Schema markup & SEO foundations. Local business, organization, service, FAQ schema as needed
  • 90+ Lighthouse score on mobile (performance, accessibility, SEO, best-practices)
  • Google Search Console & sitemap submission on launch day
  • SSL, HTTPS, fast hosting from day one
  • Editable backend — you can update content yourself without paying us

What we honestly cannot do in 48 hours: e-commerce sites with 500+ products, multi-language sites, complex booking systems with payment integrations, or sites requiring custom backend functionality beyond a contact form. Those are 5–10 day builds. We'll tell you upfront if your project is in this category.

Why we built it this way

Small businesses need to move fast. If you've decided you need a website, you usually need it because you're already losing customers without one. Telling you it'll be 8 weeks isn't acceptable when your competition can get a Wix site live tomorrow.

So we built our process to match real small-business speed: brief on Monday morning, live site by Wednesday afternoon, with the same quality you'd get from a 6-week build. The trick was redesigning the workflow, not cutting the quality.
